SoftBank, OpenAI Unveil Japan AI Joint Venture

By Codepro | February 3, 2025 | Updated 16 min ago

Source: gizmodo.com

Summary

  • SoftBank and OpenAI launch a joint venture for AI services in Japan.
  • SoftBank to pay $3 billion annually for OpenAI technology.
  • SoftBank plans to invest $15-$25 billion in OpenAI.

SoftBank Group (9984.T) CEO Masayoshi Son announced a new partnership with OpenAI CEO Sam Altman to establish SB OpenAI Japan, a joint venture to offer AI services to corporate customers.

The Japanese investment giant will allocate $3 billion annually to integrate OpenAI’s technology across its group companies.

Strategic Investments

SoftBank is considering a $15-$25 billion investment in OpenAI, further deepening its collaboration with the U.S. AI leader.

The company is also committing $15 billion to Stargate, a joint AI venture with OpenAI and Oracle aimed at enhancing AI infrastructure in the U.S.

Masayoshi Son’s AI Ambitions

Son’s renewed focus on AI follows a period of strategic retrenchment. His support for OpenAI underscores SoftBank’s commitment to being a major player in the global AI ecosystem.

SoftBank has a long history of bringing cutting-edge U.S. technology to Japan, including introducing the iPhone to the Japanese market in 2008.
