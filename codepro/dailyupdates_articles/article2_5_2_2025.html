5G spectrum auction in India

Codepro | February 4, 2025

The 5G spectrum auction in India has been delayed to July 2025. Here are some key points regarding the 5G spectrum auction:
  • 5G Spectrum Auction Delayed: The Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) is expected to submit its final recommendations to the Department of Telecommunications (DoT) by March 2025. As a result, the 5G spectrum auctions will likely take place in July 2025, rather than the previously announced April-May timeframe.
  • Previous Auctions: In July 2022, the 5G spectrum auction saw bids worth over ₹1.49 lakh crore, indicating significant interest from telecom companies.
  • Telecom Minister's Comments: Union minister Ashwini Vaishnaw has stated that the auctions in April-May are unlikely and a July timeframe is possible.
  • Industry Demand: Telecom companies have expressed a desire for an urgent rollout of 5G services, with Mukesh Ambani, chairman of Reliance Jio, calling for 5G to be made a "national priority."
  • Previous Timeline: Initially, the 5G spectrum auctions were expected to take place in February 2022, but were delayed due to various reasons.
  • 5G Trials Extension: The Department of Telecommunications (DoT) has approved the extension of 5G trials, pushing the auction to the second half of 2022 and the launch to late 2022 or early 2023.
  • Spectrum Bands: The 5G spectrum auction includes bands such as 700 Mhz, 3.5 Ghz, and 26 Ghz frequency bands.
  • Competition in Auction: During the 2022 auction, there was good competition for all bands of the spectrum, with bids coming in for 5G airwaves.
  • Service Rollout: Telecom companies have stated that they will take six months to start offering 5G services from when the spectrum is allotted.
