SoftBank, OpenAI Unveil Japan AI Joint Venturee

By Supantha Mukherjee | February 3, 2025 | Updated 28 min ago

Source: gizmodo.com

Summary

  • Several companies have switched to DeepSeek to save costs.
  • DeepSeek's structural impact is pervasive, say executives.
  • Startups are quick to switch, while large firms are cautious.

Hemanth Mandapati, CEO of German startup Novo AI, quickly adopted DeepSeek chatbots, moving away from OpenAI’s ChatGPT just two weeks ago.

"If you have built your application using OpenAI, you can easily migrate to the other ones ... it took us minutes to switch," he said at the GoWest conference in Sweden.

DeepSeek’s lower pricing has been a major attraction for companies. Startups are adopting it quickly, while larger enterprises are more cautious.

DeepSeek vs OpenAI: Cost Comparison

According to analysts at Bernstein, DeepSeek's pricing is 20 to 40 times cheaper than OpenAI's models. For example:

  • OpenAI charges $2.5 per million input tokens.
  • DeepSeek charges only $0.014 for the same number of tokens.

A Game Changer for Europe?

Europe has lagged behind the U.S. in AI investments, with only $15.8 billion invested in 2024, compared to nearly $100 billion in the U.S. However, DeepSeek’s affordability could level the playing field.

"It marks a significant step forward in democratizing AI and reducing barriers to entry," said Seena Rejal, Chief Commercial Officer of NetMind.AI.

Challenges & Regulatory Scrutiny

Despite its benefits, DeepSeek faces regulatory concerns. Some regulators are investigating whether it copies OpenAI data or censors content that could reflect negatively on China.

Future of AI Pricing

AI pricing wars have begun. Microsoft recently made OpenAI’s o1 reasoning model free for Copilot users, dropping the usual $20/month fee.

While startups are quick to switch, major companies like Nokia and SAP are waiting to see how DeepSeek addresses security and compliance concerns.
